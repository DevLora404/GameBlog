Week 2 - What to expect?

This week, there were no finished designs or major breakthroughs in Unity apart from some motion tests. I spent most of my time polishing and discarding ideas

So it's time to really give a broad context of what the game will be like and how I hope it works (if everything goes well)

The project is currently called Mobile Grave. The name might change in the future, though I don't think it will.

Let Me Break It Down

Mobile - As I mentioned last week, the RV (Recreational Vehicle) design is extremely important — not just because it’s your only means of transportation (yes, there will be no other drivable cars), but also because it's one of the few safe zones you'll find in the game.

You can think of Pacific Drive as a reference point.

I really want you to care about the RV — to see it not just as a vehicle, but as a companion.

The RV will also serve as a warehouse and a place to sleep in case the dark atmosphere of the game does not catch your attention

The RV will have a system of keys and gasoline, please take care of the battery and do not forget to close the door before leaving

Grave- The game will be difficult. You will die. There are no save points or second chances. I know this might limit the audience a bit (especially considering only about four people even know this project exists). Still, I made this decision because I’m tired of overly simple games (I won’t name any). Since there’s no difficulty selector, it’s important to understand that you’ll need to study your surroundings carefully and ask yourself: is it really worth going out? I’m still working out the types of damage the game will include (fall damage, for example), so I can’t say exactly how many hits you can take — but I can assure you that you’ll be able to upgrade your health and shielding a bit, so it doesn’t feel unfair. Oh, and yes — you can exit the game and save. I’m not that cruel.
